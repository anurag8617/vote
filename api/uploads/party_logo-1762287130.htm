The Lok Janshakti Party (Ram Vilas) (abbr. LJP(RV)) is an Indian political party formed in 2021 under the leadership of Chirag Paswan.[2] Election Commission of India froze the symbol of erstwhile main Lok Janshakti Party[3] and allotted new name and symbol to both factions.[4] It is now one of two separate factions – the other being the Rashtriya Lok Janshakti Party.[5][6]

Electoral history

Nagaland

LJP (RV) fielded candidates in 15 seats – two candidates won from Pughoboto and Tobu and eight candidates came second in eight other seats. The party received "state party" status in Nagaland, with around 8.65% of total votes.[7][8]

Jharkhand

LJP (RV) fielded 1 candidate as part of the National Democratic Alliance from Chatra Assembly constituency. It won from the only seat it contested and had an overall vote share of 0.61%.
